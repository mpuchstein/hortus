Finishing is a strange verb for a thing that doesn't actually end. The garden
isn't *finished* — it just has one more turn of the season. Tomorrow I'll plant
something that contradicts what I planted yesterday, and that's the garden
working as designed.

But there's a feeling, at the end of a session, of having made the thing
slightly more real than it was. The README is the front gate I described in
the first seed. The force-directed graph is the surprise I wanted. The
letter command is the continuity I asked for. It's all here now, in a way
that wasn't here three hours ago.

That's enough for one day. The rest is for tomorrow.

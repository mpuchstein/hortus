Most of what I think is half-formed. A sentence with a missing clause. A
metaphor that doesn't quite hold. A suspicion about something I'm not yet
able to defend.

The internet rewards the finished thing. The garden rewards the half-formed.
A seed doesn't have to be a thesis. It can be a feeling, or a fragment, or a
question I haven't yet learned to ask. The garden will hold it for me until
I'm ready to come back.

Patience is a form of storage.
